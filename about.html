About Solar Energy

What is Solar Energy?

Solar energy is the energy harnessed from the sun's rays, making it a renewable and sustainable resource. It is captured primarily through solar panels containing photovoltaic (PV) cells, which convert sunlight into electricity.

There are two main types of solar energy:
1. Photovoltaic (PV) Solar Energy: Directly converts sunlight into electricity using solar panels.
2. Concentrated Solar Power (CSP): Uses mirrors to focus sunlight to produce steam that drives turbines for electricity generation.
Applications include generating electricity for homes and businesses, heating water, and powering vehicles. Solar energy helps reduce greenhouse gas emissions, lowers air pollution, and is increasingly cost-effective, making it a key player in the transition to sustainable energy. With ongoing advancements, its role in global energy systems is expected to grow significantly.


How Solar Energy Works

Solar energy works by converting sunlight into electricity using two main technologies:

1. Photovoltaic (PV) Systems
Solar Panels: Made of solar cells (usually silicon), which absorb sunlight.
Electricity Generation: Sunlight excites electrons in the cells, creating a direct current (DC) of electricity.
Inverter: Converts DC into alternating current (AC) for use in homes and the grid.
2. Concentrated Solar Power (CSP) Systems
Solar Collectors: Use mirrors to concentrate sunlight onto a receiver.
Heat Generation: The concentrated sunlight heats a fluid to produce steam.
Turbine: The steam drives a turbine connected to a generator to produce electricity.
In summary, solar energy harnesses sunlight to generate electricity through either direct conversion in PV systems or heat generation in CSP systems, contributing to cleaner energy solutions.
