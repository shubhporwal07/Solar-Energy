Challenges in Solar Energy Adoption

Challenges in solar energy adoption include:
1.High Initial Costs: Upfront expenses for solar panels and installation can be prohibitive for many
2.Intermittency: Solar energy generation varies with weather and time, necessitating reliable storage or backup systems.
3.Land Use: Large solar farms require significant land, potentially leading to conflicts in densely populated area.
4.Regulatory Barriers: Complex permitting and inconsistent regulations can delay projects.
5.Grid Integration: Incorporating solar into existing power grids poses technical challenges for stability.
For more details, visit MIT Climate Portal and IEA - Solar PV.
